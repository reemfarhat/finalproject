Black identities are often siloed and boxed in ways that create one dimensional representations. But Black identities are complex. Craig Newmark’s Photojournalism II course shot a series of photoessays that depict cultural, economic, and social scenes within Black communities in New York City. These photoessays tell compelling visual stories that highlight how deeply rich and diverse the Black experience is. Youcef Bounab’s photos focus on Black Muslims in the Bronx and their observance of Ramadan, Briana Ellis-Gibbs explores Black people’s relationship to their skin color and colorism, Jimmie McKinnie and Uvie Bikomo examines the blackfishing phenomenon online, Chery Dieu Nalio looks at how Haitian American painters are preserving their identities in New York, and finally Brianna Monsanto created a photo series that highlights alternative Black identities through “The Other Yearbook.”
